What is this component?

We know that our users needs are not being met currently by the existing progress indicator. From including a visual progress indicator we observed our users needs to see their progress in an assessment being met much clearer.

Dependencies

The SCSS used for the progress bar is bespoke, and therefore has no dependencies.

Research

Background

We’ve observed that a % complete indicator, accompanied by a progress bar, help the user to form an estimate of approximately how long is left, without being daunting. The task list and step-by-step navigation aren’t applicable to specific user need that this pattern seeks to meet. These patterns apply to a multi-step process consisting of discrete tasks, which can be completed in a linear (step-by-step navigation) or non-linear (task list) manner. Whereas our product is a single, discrete task (consisting of multiple ‘steps’ i.e. statements to respond to).

Research findings

From discovery, we knew the existing pattern of “Question X of Y” wasn’t working. It caused a significant negative reaction in our users, contributing to only 1.6% of the existing service’s visitors finishing an assessment. We know some users who visit our service are at a difficult point in their life (such as being made redundant, or being involuntarily long-term unemployed), and we know that a users emotional state alters their perception of time, so seeing Question 1 of 56, was off-putting for a significant amount of users we researched with. We conducted further research to examine the impact of removing a progress indicator altogether.

We found there was a clear need to include something. Users wanted to see a visual progress indicator which helped to engage them and keep them informed of their progress visually as well as numerically. We knew that implementing either a progress bar or a numerical indicator in isolation wasn’t going to work.

The need of a progress bar was a running theme across various sprints.

Theme: ‘Users didn’t like that they didn’t know how long was left of the assessment’
