When to use

An alternate standard hero banner for situations where there may be multiple pages which require a prominent piece of content at the top of the page.

The example shown above shows the snippets use on Explore careers at the top of the job profile. Content found within this preview is available through other snippets. The code provided simply provides the hero wrapper.

Research

Background

The purpose of this type of banner is to introduce users to the job profile and provide some high level information. It was evaluated the majority of the time we went through various tasks for the job profile content. All users understood what the page is about and no issues were reported in understanding the information underneath the main title as supporting (alternative titles in our case)

Research findings

A number of user needs such as the salary, working hours and patterns were identified from users as to be the most important. We decided instead of https://open.spotify.com/track/5e7ux6X0uNokUL30hetmJe?si=hB2aVoirTlCrGbDMgBQ0pwhaving that information in the main body of the page to introduce it at a more prominent position on the top.

Each snippet of information contains different infographics which are relevant to the information we are representing and were evaluated separately and in combination

As salary was the most critical element it was given more prominence space in relation to the other elements but on the other hand all information is kept on one line on desktop to show equal significance. In mobile view, salary is on top following typical hours, you could work. In all scenarios users were able to find the relevant information.
